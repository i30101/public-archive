Andrew Kim, Vice President
Grade: Junior
About me: Andrew is a rising Junior who has a passion for all things related to Science Olympiad. When he's not running or playing the piano, you'll find Andrew working on Scioly, cramming notes into oversized binders or writing scripts that make practice tests. His favorite event for the 2023 season was Environmental Chemistry. If he didn't like Scioly as much, he'd be reading more Isaac Asimov and traveling to Alaska.
Favorite thing about Woodson Scioly: Medalling, spending time with the team, and going to cool places. This season, my fondest memory was staying up before States, studying and eating Grass Jelly with two other team members in our hotel room, and earning 4 medals the next day. My least favorite part of Scioly is how my teammates tend to snap goofy pictures of me at competitions when I'm yawning or sleeping.
Zahra Ramakdawala, Secretary
Grade: Senior
About me: I'm a rising senior who loves Science Olympiad almost as much as I love Temm's hyper realistic frog drawings. With a speciality in bio and life science events, you'd likely find me fitting one last diagram into my 5pt font Anatomy notes or memorizing one more random history fact for Disease Detectives. If I'm not studying for Scioly, you could find me rowing as captain on the women's crew team or listening to SEB on Spotify.
Favorite thing about Woodson Scioly: When one of the in-person invitationals was canceled and rescheduled to be online, my partner and I decided to have some fun and make dinner before taking the tests. On the day we decided to take the tests, we spent two hours cooking a full three course meal. After dinner, we got ready to take the test and they hadn't even been assigned on scilympiad. We then stayed up for another 5 hours watching horror movies, listening to Icelandic metal, and finishing our forestry notes. Close second was when me and Temm medaled at the Princeton invitational and we decided to walk out of the cathedral after getting our medal (instead of sitting back down) and I tripped on the way out and everyone stared at us.
